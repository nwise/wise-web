Remember what you know

 "Remember what you know."

 
I picked up that piece of advice this week.  It's helped me gain a little perspective in what is becoming a tumultuous time in my life.  Especially now during the holidays it seems that things are increasingly chaotic.  There seems to be so much pressure to make Christmas a memorable event, that we miss the point.  A good friend of mine refers to this phenomenon as "hurry up and have fun".  
 
I'm not interested in making every minute count.  Remember what you know.  What I know is that when I become a slave to my schedule and pressure myself to 'make the most' of my time, I miss out on a lot of opportunities to just enjoy people in my life.  Relationships are not something that fits a timeline or fills a time slot on a schedule.
 
I think the best thing you can do this Christmas is let go of your schedule.  Relax and enjoy your situation.  Enjoy your family.  Just because you can accomplish a lot, doesn't mean you should.  Consider whether your achievements will be as rewarding as your relationships.  
 
"These wonderful things are the things we remember all through our lives"; Remember what you know.
